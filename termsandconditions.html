Crisis

Corrigan Psychology Ltd is not able to provide crisis mental health support. Clients who are suicidal or in crisis, or who require urgent attention in relation to their mental health are advised to contact their GP surgery, NHS 24 (111), 999 or local emergency services. If your GP surgery is closed, NHS 24 is available to give help and advice or the Samaritans (116 123) provide twenty-four hour support to people who are in crisis or suicidal.

Cancellation, Non-attendance & Late attendance

Any self-funded or private healthcare provider funded session cancelled or rescheduled by the Client with less than forty-eight hours' notice will be charged in full to the Client. Sessions missed without notice of cancellation are charged in full. Sessions unable to proceed due to technical issues on the Client’s part are charged in full. Clients logging in part-way through their allotted time will be seen to the end of the allotted time only, and the full session fee will apply. All appointments for uninsured Clients (self-funding) must be paid at the time of booking the appointment or at least twenty-four hours before the appointment. Failure to do so will result in the Client forfeiting the session.

Occasionally, technical challenges can affect any service and would typically be beyond the Psychologist’s control. Connection issues causing significant disruption to a session would be continued instead by telephone.

Personal Information

Corrigan Psychology Ltd abides by the code of confidentiality of HCPC and GDPR regulations. Identifying information, such as Client’s name, address, biographical details and other description of a Client’s life and his/her circumstances, will be kept private and stored digitally on an encrypted system.

Any information noted on paper as part of clinical notes during sessions will be transferred onto an encrypted system, and hard copies will be shredded. Client information will continue to be stored securely for seven years after therapy comes to an end. The Client may request to access their personal information at any time.

Confidentiality and Records

The full Privacy Policy for Corrigan Psychology Ltd can be viewed at corriganpsychology.co.uk/privacypolicy. The privacy of Clients accessing services is protected from the point of initial enquiry to the end of therapy, and afterwards. What is discussed in therapy is confidential between Client and Psychologist. Brief session notes are kept and stored securely. If it is assessed that a Client is at risk of harming themselves, or a disclosure is made that leads the Psychologist to think that someone else is at risk of coming to harm, then information will be shared with the relevant agencies. Sometimes it is useful or necessary for the Psychologist to be in touch with a Client’s GP. The Psychologist may discuss contacting the Client’s GP with the Client, depending on what arises in assessment and therapy. Psychologists receive clinical supervision where cases are discussed to ensure quality and safe practice. Client identity is protected in supervision. Supervision is bound by a confidentiality agreement.

Session Conduct

Appropriate modes of dress are required, as if you were attending the appointment in person. The Psychologist reserves the right to terminate a session or withdraw from the provision of treatment in cases where the Psychologist considers behaviour or attire to be inappropriate; or the Client engages in abusive, threatening, or harassing behaviour; or presents under the influence of alcohol or non-prescribed drugs. Such conduct may also result in permanent discontinuation of the therapeutic relationship. This policy ensures the safety and well-being of both parties and upholds the standards of ethical practice. Recording of video sessions is not permitted without prior agreement in writing with the Psychologist.
